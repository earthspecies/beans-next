BEANS-Next and ROOTS
Bioacoustics and ethology encompass a wide range of audio understanding tasks, many of which stand to benefit from recent advances in large audio-language models. However, progress in the field has so far been assessed on a narrow set of tasks, primarily centered on label-centric biological category recognition, such as species and call-type classification. In this work, we introduce BEANS-Next, a benchmark grounded in a taxonomy of bioacoustics tasks spanning acoustic perception, biological category recognition, structural and temporal reasoning, and in-context learning. Using BEANS-Next, we show that existing models exhibit limited performance beyond the task families emphasized by existing evaluations, constraining their usefulness for broader bioacoustic applications. To support progress on this broader task space, we also introduce ROOTS, a large-scale training resource built from expanded curated real-world data and previously underused behavioral and acoustic metadata, supplemented by audio-derived information and scalable synthetic generation where labeling is insufficient. We demonstrate that training on this dataset yields substantial progress across all task groups of BEANS-Next, moving audio-language models closer to their potential as general-purpose assistants for bioacoustics and ethology. To accelerate progress in the field, we open-source our benchmark, dataset, and data pipelines.

Overview

Existing bioacoustic machine learning benchmarks primarily evaluate species classification and narrow semantic recognition tasks. However, real-world ethology workflows require substantially broader capabilities, including acoustic measurement, temporal reasoning, call counting, structural comparison, and adaptation to novel taxa or user-defined sound classes.

BEANS-Next introduces a taxonomy and benchmark for evaluating these capabilities, while ROOTS provides a large-scale training dataset constructed from curated bioacoustic archives, metadata-grounded synthesis, and synthetic audio pipelines.

🧪 BEANS-Next

Benchmark covering acoustic perception, semantic recognition, structural reasoning, and in-context learning.

🌱 ROOTS

Large-scale audio-language training dataset aligned with the benchmark taxonomy.

🦜 Ethology-Centered

Designed around real bioacoustic research workflows rather than narrow classification-only evaluation.

BEANS-Next Taxonomy

Tier 1

Acoustic Perception

Fundamental frequency, duration, acoustic descriptors, and fine-grained perceptual reasoning.

Tier 2

Semantic Recognition

Species recognition, taxonomy prediction, call-type classification, and life-stage prediction.

Tier 3

Structural Reasoning

Counting, temporal structure, vocal ordering, diarization, and relational analysis.

Tier 4

In-Context Learning

Adaptation to novel taxa, user-defined classes, and multi-audio reasoning.
